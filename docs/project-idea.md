# Project Idea

## Background

Most people only begin thinking seriously about elder care and retirement living when a health event forces the issue — either their own or a parent's. By that point, options are limited, costs are high, and financial preparation is minimal or nonexistent. At the same time, young professionals rarely have a structured way to save specifically for elder care, as opposed to general retirement savings.

## Problem

- Elder care and quality assisted living are expensive and often unaffordable when the need arises suddenly.
- Financial planning tools and elder care services exist in silos — there is no platform that connects long-term investment directly to real care outcomes.
- Families are often left to research elder care options alone, without transparent pricing or verified providers.
- There is little visibility into how today's savings translate into tomorrow's care choices.

## Proposed Solution

ElderCare-Invest is a platform that lets individuals start a dedicated, goal-based investment plan for elder care from a young age. The platform will:

- Allow users to create long-term investment plans specifically earmarked for elder care.
- Provide a marketplace connecting users to partnered elder care facilities, healthcare providers, and insurance companies.
- Give users visibility into their investment growth relative to their elder care goals through a portfolio dashboard.
- Centralize healthcare plans, insurance policies, and important documents in one secure place.
- Send reminders and generate reports to help users stay on track over the decades-long planning horizon.

## Expected Benefits

- **For individuals and families:** A structured, transparent way to plan and pay for elder care well in advance, reducing financial stress later in life.
- **For elder care organizations:** A pipeline of financially prepared future residents/clients, and a channel to reach younger audiences early.
- **For insurance and healthcare providers:** A platform to offer relevant products to users who are already actively planning for long-term care.
- **For society:** Encouraging early financial planning for aging, which can reduce reliance on emergency or underfunded care arrangements later in life.
